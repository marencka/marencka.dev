# Hello!

Welcome to my application for the Design Engineer position at Tailwind Labs.

## Who am I?

I am currently a developer and designer living in the Pacific Northwest around the Seattle area. I am an avid reader, tinkerer, knitter and sports fan. In my free time I like to work on my passion project, Himmel, and go on long walks.
## My story

Growing up in rural Montana, I had little access to proper STEM education and had no conception of what computer science or software engineering entailed. The stable computer and internet connection in my early years came from the public libraries archaic computer that ran Windows 98, and from here, I fell in love with technology.

I arrived at UNC-Chapel Hill as a biochemistry major and worked at the emergency department at night for 2.5 years. While I loved the science in medicine, I struggled with the intracacies of the American healthcare system. During my various night shifts, I stumbled upon a Reddit thread on how to build your own computer. I was hooked. I began saving up for parts that night.

After the joy of building my computer and my frustration with American healthcare, I decided to change my major to Computer Science at the last minute as a Junior. I did not know a line of code or anything about software engineering, I just had a gut feeling this was the right path for me.

At the end of my senior year, after stuffing my schedule to the brim with computer science classes, I realized it was time to build something. I built this website in homage to the first computer I fell in love with at the public library. This website was my passion project and ultimately caught the eyes of Microsoft, where I ended up accepting a position.
## My work

Currently, I work as the lead front-end engineer for Designer--a web application that leverages the OpenAI DALL-E model to generate images from natural language input. I began working on Designer in late 2022, pre-launch, as the sole front-end engineer. I took it to over 5 billion images created and 3 million daily users. We even were a part of a superbowl commercial!

Additionally, due to lack of team resources, I am also the only UX/UI designer for this product. Due to this unique position of designer & engineer, I have become obsessive about design. My role has honed my attention to detail, earning me the nickname “pixel stickler” on my team.

As Designer gained popularity I lead the technical onboarding of several partner groups across the organization such as SwiftKey and Skype. Ultimately, I integrated the customer-facing experience into flagship products such as Bing and Copilot. As I helped integrate these partners I strengthened both my written and verbal communication skills and learned nuance around the differences between communicating to inform and communicating to teach.
## My passion project

How does one grade a sunset? Is it the vividity? Vibrance? Should you add extra points for the awe-inspiring clouds? These are some of the questions that I have been toiling with while creating Himmel, an app that predicts sunrise and sunset scores based on weather data using a neural network.

Being able to have creative freedom and domain over Himmel has been huge for me. Himmel has allowed me to learn about exciting new technologies such as Three.js, animations, and computational photography. It has also provided me my first hands-on experience with implementing a neural network model and the feature engineering that comes with that.

Himmel is my art piece to the world. Like Tailwind, I am not putting revenue first by spamming the user with ugly ads. I want this app to feel like a sunset itself -- understated, quiet, and simple yet beautiful.
## Why this position?

I am grateful for the unique skills and experiences that my position at Microsoft has provided, but I am eager to explore a role that offers more creative design freedom. I deeply care about the products I create and strive to have a significant impact on the user experience.

I want to have a domain over what I create. I want to be able to polish every pixel, iterate on feedback and be on the forefront of web technology. I want to work for a company that is passionate about the product and its people, not just revenue.

I am confident that Tailwind Labs is that company, and I am eager to contribute my skills and experiences to your team.

Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to the possibility of working with you and am available to answer any questions you may have.

Thank you,
Alexis
